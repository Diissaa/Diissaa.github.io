Recipe #1: Hummus with Tahini

This recipe is easy to follow and will give you the creamiest Hummus. Provided by the Mediterranean Dish.

  • Time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 8 people
Ingredients Quantity
Cooked Chickpeas 3 cups
Garlic 2 Cloves
Ice Cubes 3 to 4
Tahini Paste 1/3 cup
Kosher Salt 1/2 tsp
Extra Virgin Olive Oil As desired

Step 1: Add chickpeas and minced garlic to the bowl of a food processor. Puree until a smooth, powder-like mixture forms.

Step 2: While processor is running, add ice cubes, tahini, salt, and lemon juice. Blend for about 4 minutes or so. Check, and if the consistency is too thick still, run processor and slowly add a little hot water. Blend until you reach desired silky smooth consistency.

Step 3: Spread in a serving bowl and add a generous drizzle of olive oil. Add a few chickpeas to the middle, if you like. Sprinkle sumac on top. Enjoy with warm pita wedges and your favorite veggies.

Recipe #2: Guacamole

The perfect party dip. Follow this recipe from My Latina Table for a delicious treat.

  • Time: 10 minutes
  • Serves: 3 People
Ingredients Quantity
Avocado 2
Onion 1/4
Jalapeno 1
Cilantro 1/4 cup
Lime Juice 1-2 tsp
Tomato 1-2

Step 1: Peel the avocado and remove the core.

Step 2: Mash the avocado in a molcajete until it reaches your desired consistency.

Step 3: Add the onion, jalapeno, cilantro and tomato and mix well.

Step 4: Add lime juice and salt to taste.

Recipe #3: Tzatiki Sauce

An easy and versatile greek recipe that will go with almost anything! Thanks to The Mediterranean Dish for this quick and easy recipe.

  • Time: 10 minutes
  • Serves: 2 cups
Ingredients Quantity
Cucumber 3/4
Garlic 3 cloves
White vinegar 1 tsp
Greek yogurt 2 cups
Ground white pepperr 1/4 tsp
Olive Oil 1 tbs

Step 1: Use a box grater to manually grate the cucumbers or you can use a small food processor to finely chop the cucumbers as I do in this recipe. Toss the grated cucumbers with ½ teaspoon kosher salt. Spoon the grated cucumber into a cheese cloth or a double thickness napkin and squeeze dry. (There will be a lot of liquid).

Step 2: In one large mixing bowl, place the garlic with remaining ½ teaspoon salt, white vinegar, and extra virgin olive oil. Mix to combine.

Step 3: Add the grated cucumber to the bowl with the garlic mixture. Stir in the yogurt, and a pinch of white or black pepper, and the fresh herbs (if using. I did not use fresh herbs in the video). Stir to combine well.

Step 4: Cover and refrigerate for a bit (anywhere from 30 minutes to a couple hours before serving). This will help thicken the sauce and give it the best texture, while allowing the flavors to meld.

Step 5: When ready to serve, stir the tzatziki sauce to refresh and transfer to serving bowl, drizzle with more extra virgin olive oil, if you like. Serve with your favorite veggies, pita chips or wedges.
